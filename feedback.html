Fisherman Feedback — Yellowedge Grouper

Survey comments, sentiment scoring, and geography-aware summaries (Feb 2024)

Table of Contents

    Executive Summary

    Situation: The Council received open-ended comments (Sept 15–Oct 13, 2023) about Yellowedge Grouper. Leaders needed a quick, defensible read on sentiment by sector and where concerns clustered geographically.

    Task: Clean comments, reconcile manual vs. automated sentiment, and produce geo-aware rollups using a 21-area Gulf grid—handling multi-area responses correctly.

    Action:

    • Built a lightweight R pipeline to tokenize text, apply a customized Bing lexicon, and compare with dual-reviewer manual labels.
    • Exploded multi-area selections to area-level records; aggregated counts and polarity by sector and grid cell.
    • Generated clean visuals (bars/maps/coef-style plots) suitable for briefings.

    Result: A concise, reproducible sentiment + geography report showing where positions concentrate and which keywords drive polarity—supporting quicker, better-targeted engagement.

    Overview

    The pipeline converts 63 analyzed comments into sector and geography summaries with both manual and lexicon-based sentiment. For portfolio purposes, emphasis is on the workflow: robust text cleaning, polarity reconciliation, and careful handling of multi-area responses for map-ready outputs.

    • Sample: 63 comments with sector tags; locations via 21-area Gulf grid (multi-select allowed).
    • Sentiment: Two human reviewers (consensus labels) + automated lexicon scoring with fisheries terms (e.g., “depredation”, “longline”).
    • Geo: Area-level rollups after exploding multi-area selections (map totals can exceed N).

    Methods Snapshot

    From raw text to actionable, geo-enabled insights:

    • Text processing: Tokenization, stopword removal, custom lexicon merge; manual labels for calibration.
    • Polarity reconciliation: Compare manual vs. auto; flag conflicts; prioritize human consensus.
    • Geography: Separate rows by selected areas; aggregate counts/polarity by area and sector.
    • Tooling: R (tidyverse, tidytext), map-ready tables, ggplot2 charts.

    Highlights

    • Manual + automated sentiment gives both quality control and scale.
    • Geo-aware rollups respect multi-area responses—no double-counting at the comment level.
    • Keyword driver view clarifies why sentiment skews positive or negative.
    • Short, reproducible script regenerates all tables/figures.

    Results Snapshot

    Figures progress from participation and sentiment by sector → keyword drivers → geo density and geo polarity → topic subset views.

    Geo Distribution of Responses

    Map of responses across 21 Gulf areas. Because respondents could select multiple areas, the total map count (n=185) exceeds the number of individuals. This ensures multi-area tagging was correctly expanded for analysis rather than under- or over-counted.

    • Data integrity: Multi-area responses exploded into area-level rows for accurate rollups.
    • Spatial awareness: Distribution shows where concerns cluster across the Gulf.
    • Workflow: Map-ready tables generated directly from cleaned survey data for briefing use.

    This step demonstrates geography-aware data engineering, where text survey responses are prepared for clear, stakeholder-ready spatial summaries.

    Sentiment by Fishing Sector

    Open-ended survey comments were classified into positive, negative, and neutral sentiment and cross-tabulated by sector. Respondents could select multiple sectors, so totals exceed the number of individuals (n=73).

    • Commercial: Mostly negative, with only limited positive sentiment.
    • Private recreational: Broader mix—negative and neutral dominate, but positives appear as well.
    • For-hire: Smaller sample, sentiment balanced across categories.

    This illustrates the ability to turn unstructured feedback into structured, stakeholder-level insights. The workflow ensures that managers can see at a glance where support or resistance is strongest.

    Sentiment by Area

    Each grid cell displays the mix of sentiment (positive/neutral/negative) and the local sample size. Comments were labeled by two reviewers and then linked to one or more self-reported areas; multi-area responses were expanded to area-level rows (map total n=185 > 63 comments).

    • Geography-aware: Correctly handles multi-select locations to avoid under/over-counting.
    • Apportionment: Per-area sentiment helps target outreach where views skew supportive vs. critical.
    • Data quality: Small-N cells are visible at a glance, discouraging over-interpretation.

    This figure demonstrates clean data engineering + clear spatial storytelling—turning open-text feedback into map-ready, decision-focused summaries.

    Word Cloud: Sentiment Drivers

    Word frequency visualization highlights the keywords driving sentiment in comments. Positive terms (green) emphasize stock health and abundance, while negative terms (orange) point to limits, loss, and difficulty.

    • Positive themes: “healthy,” “plenty,” “large,” “sustainable.”
    • Negative themes: “limits,” “loss,” “hard,” “impossible.”
    • Balance: Both optimism about stock health and frustration with constraints emerge clearly.

    This layer translates raw comments into actionable signals about what words and ideas stakeholders most associate with stock condition—helping prioritize communication points.

    What This Demonstrates

    • Text-to-insight: Convert messy comments into interpretable, quality-controlled sentiment.
    • Geo-aware analysis: Correctly handle multi-area tagging for map-ready rollups.
    • Reproducibility: Script-first workflow that a policy team can rerun and audit.
    • Communication: Clear visuals that move fast from data to decisions.
